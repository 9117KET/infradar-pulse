# InfradarAI — Traction & Marketing Knowledge Base

Derived from applying the Bullseye Framework (Weinberg & Mares, "Traction") to InfradarAI's stage, market, and product capabilities. This is a strategic reference document — not a feature spec. Use it to run channel experiments, prep sales calls, and target partners.

---

## The Core Principle: 50/50 Rule

Split time equally between building the product and building traction. At pre-scale B2B, distribution beats product quality. A slightly worse product with excellent distribution wins.

**Applies to InfradarAI because:** The product is already well-built. The bottleneck is not features — it is getting the right 10 people to pay, then the right 100.

---

## Bullseye Framework Applied to InfradarAI

### Middle Ring — 3 Channels to Experiment With First

These three are highest-probability at zero customers, zero brand, and low budget:

**1. Direct Sales**
- Why: You can start today with no budget. High-touch outreach to named target buyers.
- What works: Personalized cold email with a custom InfradarAI research PDF attached (use the Research Hub to generate it).
- Success metric: Demo → Paid conversion rate >20% over 4 weeks.
- Kill signal: <10% show rate after 20 outreach sequences.

**2. Business Development (Partnerships)**
- Why: A single BD deal with a consulting firm or investment bank compresses months of individual sales into weeks.
- What works: Find one champion inside a KPMG/Deloitte/Macquarie who sees InfradarAI as a tool they can resell or recommend internally.
- Success metric: 1 signed pilot deal with a partner org within 8 weeks.
- Kill signal: 3+ partner intros → 0 demos requested → move on.

**3. Content Marketing (Public Intelligence Reports)**
- Why: Publishing a weekly public snippet of InfradarAI's data proves the signal quality without requiring a sales call.
- What works: 1-page PDF or web page with "$X billion in new African infrastructure this week" + 3 specific project teasers + "join free to see all."
- Success metric: 50+ newsletter subscribers/week by week 6.
- Kill signal: <5 newsletter signups/week after 3 published snapshots.

### Inner Ring — Commit After Experiments

Do not commit full resources until one channel passes its success metric. Run all three in parallel for 4 weeks. Double down on the winner.

## Target Buyer Profiles

### Buyer 1: DFI Analyst

**Who they are:** Work at World Bank, IFC, AfDB, ADB, EBRD, IADB. Manage portfolios of 50-300+ projects. Responsible for monitoring project health, flagging risks, preparing briefings for leadership.

**Pain:** Data is fragmented across MDB portals, Excel files, and informal networks. Getting a clear picture of all active projects in a region takes days.

**What InfradarAI sells them:** Unified portfolio view across all 5 MDBs, confidence-scored risk signals, automated alerts when projects deviate from expected trajectory.

**What to say in outreach:**
> "I noticed you cover infrastructure in [Region]. We ingest live data from World Bank, IFC, AfDB, ADB, and EBRD — and run 40+ AI signals across each project. Here's a sample briefing for [one project they'd know]. Worth 20 minutes?"

**LinkedIn titles to target:**
- Infrastructure Portfolio Officer
- Senior Project Officer (Infrastructure)
- Investment Officer - Infrastructure
- DFI Analyst
- Program Manager - Infrastructure

### Buyer 2: Infrastructure Fund Manager

**Who they are:** Work at Macquarie, BlackRock Infrastructure, Meridiam, IFM Investors, pension fund infrastructure desks, sovereign wealth funds. Manage $500M-$10B+ in infrastructure assets.

**Pain:** Deal sourcing is slow; relies on bankers bringing deals. Monitoring 40+ portfolio companies across 12 countries with manual processes. Limited early warning on emerging political or regulatory risk.

**What InfradarAI sells them:** Early-stage pipeline (MDB data gives 12-24 months lead time vs. public tenders), risk alerts on portfolio companies, stakeholder intelligence, comparative deal comps.

**What to say in outreach:**
> "We track early-stage infrastructure pipeline across Africa and Asia — projects at MDB approval stage before they become public tenders. Attached is a sample briefing for [region/sector they focus on]. Would this kind of lead time be useful?"

**LinkedIn titles to target:**
- Infrastructure Investment Manager
- Portfolio Manager - Infrastructure

- Senior Associate - Infrastructure

### Buyer 3: EPC Contractor Business Development

**Who they are:** Work at Bechtel, Vinci, Bouygues, Strabag, China Communications Construction. Responsible for finding and winning large infrastructure contracts globally.

**Pain:** Tender information is fragmented across 20+ procurement portals. Competitive intelligence on who won what and for how much is slow. Resource-intensive to track every relevant tender across their focus regions.

**What InfradarAI sells them:** Automated tender monitoring, stakeholder maps for each project, contractor win-rate tracking, contact information for key decision-makers.

**What to say in outreach:**
> "We monitor infrastructure tenders across [Region] — consolidating 20+ procurement portals into one feed with AI-generated project briefs and key contact extraction. Here's a sample for [a relevant sector]. Happy to show you a demo."

**LinkedIn titles to target:**

### Buyer 4: Advisory / Consulting Firm

**Who they are:** Work at KPMG Infrastructure Advisory, Deloitte, PwC, McKinsey, Bain. Serve infrastructure clients and need current market intelligence to advise on entry strategy, risk, and deal pricing.

**Pain:** Client work requires rapid market intelligence. Existing tools are expensive and slow. Analysts spend too much time aggregating public data.

**What InfradarAI sells them:** Resellable intelligence tool for client engagements. White-label reports. Faster analyst research.

**Partnership angle:** Revenue share for enterprise referrals. Co-branded reports. Tool license for their analytics team.

## Cold Email Framework

Use the Research Hub to generate a custom 1-page PDF on a prospect's focus market before sending. Attach it. This is the differentiated move — it shows the product while pitching it.

### Structure (5 sentences)

1. **Observation:** "I noticed [Company] has active projects in [Region/Sector]..."
2. **Problem:** "Most teams tracking these rely on [IJGlobal/MEED/manual research] — which misses early-stage signals by 6-12 months."
3. **InfradarAI angle:** "We track [X] projects in [their focus area] from MDB pipeline stage, with AI-scored risk signals updated daily."
4. **Proof:** "Attached is a sample briefing we generated for [relevant project cluster or region]."
5. **CTA:** "Worth 20 minutes this week?"

### Subject line formulas

- `[Prospect company] + [Region they work in] — quick data share`
- `[Specific project they're linked to] — early signals`
- `[Their sector] pipeline, [their region] — InfradarAI sample`

### Personalization sources

- LinkedIn: check their recent posts and activity
- Company website: check their portfolio/active projects pages
- MDB portals: if they're listed as a co-financier on a project InfradarAI tracks, mention that specific project

### Follow-up cadence

- Day 1: Initial email with PDF
- Day 4: One-line follow-up ("Did the briefing come through?")
- Day 9: Value-add follow-up with one new data point from InfradarAI
- Day 16: Breakup email ("Closing the loop — happy to reconnect when timing is better")

## Referral Program Design

### User-to-User Referral

- Incentive: 1 free month of Pro for each referral who becomes a paying user
- Mechanism: Unique referral link per user (`infradarai.com?ref=CODE`)
- Communication: Show in Settings page; email notification when a referral converts

### Partner Referral (Consulting Firms, Banks)

- Incentive: 15% revenue share for the first 12 months of any enterprise deal sourced through a partner
- Mechanism: Partner gets a unique tracking link; BD pipeline tracker logs referral source
- Tier requirement: Partners must be logged in `bd_partners` table with `tier = 'consulting_firm'` or `'investment_bank'`

### Why These Numbers

- 1 month of Pro ($199) for a referral who pays ≥1 month is break-even on month 2 — acceptable at this stage when LTV is unknown
- 15% partner commission is below industry standard for B2B SaaS (20-30%) but appropriate while the product is pre-scale; revisit at $10K MRR

**Format options:**

## Notes on What to Avoid

- **Do not spread across all channels at once.** Pick 3, test, kill 2, double down on 1.
- **Do not measure vanity metrics.** Page views, Twitter followers, and app downloads do not tell you if traction is real. Measure CAC, LTV, Day-30 retention, and paying conversions.
- **Do not wait for the product to be perfect before selling.** The product is already good enough to charge for. Distribution is now the bottleneck.
- **Do not automate outreach before you have a message that works manually.** Find the email that gets a 30%+ reply rate by hand. Then automate.
- **Do not pitch features.** Pitch the outcome: "You'll know which projects are at risk 6 months before your competitors do."
