### Describe what your company does in detail

Infrastructure investors, EPC contractors, and project finance teams need real-time intelligence on trillions of dollars in globally planned and active infrastructure projects. Today, they pay $3,000 to $200,000 per year to incumbents like MEED, GlobalData, and Wood Mackenzie for quarterly PDF reports written by human analysts.

InfraRadar Pulse is an AI-native intelligence platform that replaces these reports. We aggregate real-time project data from five multilateral development banks - the World Bank, IFC, ADB, AfDB, and EBRD - run 40+ AI agents to score, alert on, and research each project, and deliver personalized intelligence to our users via a self-service dashboard.

The platform includes:
- Real-time alerting across 9 signal categories (Political, Financial, Regulatory, Supply Chain, Environmental, Construction, Stakeholder, Market, Security)
- An on-demand AI research agent that answers natural language queries across our full project database
- Confidence scoring and evidence verification for every project
- Portfolio-level monitoring with custom alert rules
- AI-generated intelligence digests every 4 hours
- Side-by-side project comparison and geospatial heat maps

We cover 14 global regions, 14 infrastructure sectors, and surface pre-tender signals from MDB pipelines up to 18 months before RFP issuance. This is intelligence that EPC contractors currently pay $30,000/year to competitors to get - we deliver it at $199/month.

### What do you understand about your business that other companies in it just don't get?

Three things incumbents fundamentally cannot do:

1. **The data is public but unstructured.** Every MDB publishes all project data for free - World Bank, IFC, ADB, AfDB, EBRD project documents are public domain. Incumbents hire human analysts to read them. We deploy AI agents. Our marginal cost of adding a new data source is near zero; theirs is another analyst hire.

2. **The buyers want self-service.** DFI analysts and EPC BD managers are sophisticated professionals who want to ask their own questions on demand, not wait for a quarterly PDF. The interaction model incumbents sell is wrong, not just expensive.

3. **Cross-MDB intelligence is the actual product.** No single incumbent aggregates across all five MDBs. World Bank watchers miss AfDB co-financing signals. ADB trackers miss IFC equity plays in the same country. Our cross-MDB co-financing graph is a feature no incumbent can easily replicate without rebuilding their data architecture from scratch.

### What's the biggest risk to your company?

Sales cycle length. Our target buyers (DFI analysts, infrastructure PE funds, EPC BD teams) work at institutions with procurement processes, IT security reviews, and long approval chains. A $199/month purchase still requires department head approval at some institutions.

We mitigate this by: (1) offering a free tier that creates individual adoption before institutional procurement; (2) targeting founder-controlled funds and smaller teams who can make unilateral tool decisions; (3) starting with consultants who have client budgets and faster purchasing.
